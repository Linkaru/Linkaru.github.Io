Osamu Tezuka

Osamu Tezuka, born on November 1928, was a Japanese manga artist, cartoonist, animator, film producer, medical doctor, and activist. Born in Osaka prefecture, his pioneering techniques earned him titles such as “the father of manga”, “the godfather of manga”, and “the god of manga”. Some even considered him the Japanese equivalent to Walt Disney, who was Tezuka’s inspiration.



Tezuka died of stomach cancer on February 9 1989 in Tokyo. His last words were “ I’m begging you, let me work!”, spoken to a nurse who tried to take away his drawing equipment. After his death his home city of Takarazuka, opened up a museum in his memory. His legacy has continued to be honored among manga artist and animators. Many other aspiring manga artist have cited Tezuka as their inspiration.



Tezuka was a descendent of Hattori Hanzo, a famous ninja and samurai who faithfully served Tokugawa Ieyasu. His son Makoto Tezuka became a film and anime director. Tezuka had many hobbies and interest. He enjoyed bug-collecting, entomology, Walt Disney, and baseball. Tezuka even met Walt Disney in person at the 1964 New York World’s Fair. Additional fact, he was even a fan of Superman.

  • Tezuka created more than 700 manga series
